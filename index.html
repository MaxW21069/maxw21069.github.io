How can death literacy help?

Inform and provide access to relevant services

Death literacy can give the patient in end-of-life care and their family the power to choose which services they will utilize in their care.

Prepare the family for what to expect

The last thing a family needs when they are grieving is reading and understanding the purpose of legal documents regarding their loved one. With an improved death literacy, the family will understand these documents prior, granting them the extra time and mental space they need to grieve healthily.

Undestand and deal with your own mortality

Everyone will pass one day. An improved death literacy will help you and your family prepare for that day, what your wishes will be once you are gone, and how to process it

Provide supports

Our website allows you to connect with, and support others in the community wherever they may need it. This can range from emotional support, to simple tasks like helping them rake the garden
