So what is death literacy?

Death literacy is the preparation and emotional understanding of mortality and an indiduals wishes for what to happen when they pass.
It includes important support regarding end-of-life matters such as:

  • The care they recieve
  • Assigning power of attorney for their estate
  • Certifying their end-of-life wishes in a will
  • Adhering to any religeous or cultural needs a person has
  • Emotional and psychological awareness of mortality

What is the history of death literacy?

Death literacy has been practiced for thousands of years, with each culture having their own form of understanding of mortality.
Many ancient civilizations such as the Greeks and Egyptians had rituals surrounding a person's passing, where their life was celebrated. There were fewer medical interventions regarding death in those days, most people passed at home, surrounded by family, making the process a very bittersweet, emotional experience.
When religeon became more prominant across the world, death literacy was still practiced, but in a different way. People were comforted when their time was coming, but there was a much bigger emphasis on having lived a life that aligned with their religeous values, and therefore the person being accepted into whatever awaited them according to their religeon's teachings.
